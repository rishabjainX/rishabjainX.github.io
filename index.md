Hi — I'm Rishab, a junior at Washington High School in Fremont, California.

I work at the intersection of **machine learning and policy**, with a particular focus on
**AI governance** and **algorithmic fairness** in the criminal-legal system. Most of my
research applies explainable AI to questions about how automated decisions affect young
people: from recidivism risk-assessment models to alternatives to punitive school discipline.

Outside the lab, I serve as the sole student board member on the **Fremont Unified School
District Board of Education**, representing 32,000+ students, and I co-founded the
**California Student Board Member Association** to build a state-wide network of student
trustees. I compete in Parliamentary debate, teach kids to code, and earned my first-degree
black belt in taekwondo.
